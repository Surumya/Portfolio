# Project: Spotify Daylist Optimization Strategy

**Role:** Product Manager (Assignment by Surumya Sharma)
**Objective:** Understand low adoption of Daylist and evolve it to feel more trustworthy, useful, and habitual.

## 1. Problem Statement
While Daylist is dynamic and context-aware, adoption remains low. Users revert to manual searches or static playlists (Discover Weekly) because Daylist has not become the "default" listening choice.

## 2. The Framework: Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)
- **Morning/Work Day:** "When I feel low, I want Daylist to play calm meditation music so I can feel peaceful."
- **Commute/Travel:** "When I am traveling, I want upbeat songs so I can stay energetic."
- **Focus/Work:** "When I am working, I want lo-fi or instrumental music to avoid distractions."

## 3. Key Insights (First Principles & 5 Whys)
- **The Trust Gap:** When recommendations feel wrong (e.g., upbeat music on a slow morning), users lose trust in the algorithm.
- **Predictability vs. Variety:** Users find the refresh cycle too rigid or the songs too repetitive.
- **The Root Cause:** Daylist relies on "the clock" rather than the user's actual emotional state.

## 4. Proposed Solution: Enhanced Personalized Daylist
- **Mood Input:** Allow users to provide direct input on their current mood to suggest songs they *actually* want.
- **Artist Analysis:** Backend system analyzes preferred singers for specific genres to ensure recommendation accuracy.
- **Feedback Loop:** Continuous updates based on real-time feedback to refine the user profile.

## 5. Implementation Summary
- **Technical Validation:** Align backend configurations with user mood signals.
- **Experience Shift:** Moving from time-aware to **emotion-aware** recommendations.

## 6. Success Metrics (KPIs)
- **North Star:** % Increase in Repeat Usage of Daylist within 7 days.
- **Trust Metric:** Reduction in "Skip Rate" within the first 3 songs of a Daylist.
